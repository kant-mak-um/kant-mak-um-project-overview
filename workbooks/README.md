# Workbooks

This folder contains **spreadsheet‑based working artifacts** used by the
Kant‑Mak‑Um coordination system.

Workbooks support **planning, tracking, and reflection**.  
They are *tools*, not records of authority.

---

## Purpose

Workbooks exist to:

- Make execution visible
- Reduce cognitive load
- Support weekly and daily planning
- Capture short‑term operational state

They are designed to be **edited frequently** and **replaced over time**.

---

## What Belongs Here

Typical contents include:

- Weekly dashboards
- Daily or travel‑day planners
- Academic tracking sheets
- Lightweight logs or checklists
- Temporary analysis or modeling sheets

Workbooks may be created, modified, duplicated, or discarded as needed.

---

## What Does *Not* Belong Here

This folder does **not** contain:

- Governance documents
- Long‑term plans or roadmaps
- Design specifications
- Manufacturing or product data
- Decisions of record

Authoritative decisions belong in `decisions/`.  
Structural rules belong in `docs/`.

---

## Lifecycle and Discipline

- Workbooks are **ephemeral**
- They may be superseded without ceremony
- Historical versions are not required unless explicitly needed
- If a workbook becomes stable or important, it should be documented elsewhere

The system favors **usefulness over preservation**.

---

## Relationship to Other Sections

- **02_control/** defines *what kinds* of workbooks exist conceptually
- **01_operating-system/** defines *how* they are used
- **decisions/** captures outcomes, not the workbooks themselves

Workbooks support the system but do not define it.

---

## Guiding Principle

> **Clarity over completeness.**  
> **Usefulness over permanence.**

Workbooks exist to help work move forward—not to become artifacts themselves.
