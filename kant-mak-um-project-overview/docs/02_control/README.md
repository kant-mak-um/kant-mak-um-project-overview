# Control Layer

This folder defines the **execution control layer** for the Kant‑Mak‑Um ecosystem.

If:
- `00_governance` defines *what must be true*, and
- `01_operating-system` defines *how we behave*,

then the **control layer** defines the **artifacts that make behavior visible and repeatable**.

This layer exists to support execution without bureaucracy.

---

## Purpose of the Control Layer

The control layer exists to:

1. Translate intent into visible execution
2. Provide lightweight feedback loops
3. Enable review without blame or friction
4. Preserve momentum across many parallel efforts

The control layer does **not** manage people or enforce compliance.
It provides *clarity* and *situational awareness*.

---

## What Lives in This Layer

The control layer defines **three classes of artifacts**:

### 1. Dashboards

Dashboards provide a **weekly snapshot** of reality.

They answer:
- What is the current focus?
- What is blocked or constrained?
- What advanced this week?
- What was deprioritized?

Dashboards are:
- Short‑lived
- Revised weekly
- Disposable after review

They are not historical records.

---

### 2. Checklists

Checklists support **consistent execution**, not micromanagement.

They exist to:
- Reduce cognitive load
- Prevent omission of critical steps
- Enable work to resume after interruption

Checklists may exist at different scales:
- Daily anchors
- Travel‑day variants
- Weekly review prompts

Checklists are allowed to evolve freely.

---

### 3. Review Artifacts

Review artifacts capture **learning**, not performance judgment.

They answer:
- What worked?
- What didn’t?
- What should change next cycle?

Reviews end with a **reset**, not a backlog.

Review notes are recorded separately under `decisions/`.

---

## What Explicitly Does *Not* Live Here

The control layer does **not** contain:

- Governance rules
- Long‑term plans or roadmaps
- Detailed project documentation
- Academic content
- Product or manufacturing artifacts

Those belong in their respective sections.

---

## Relationship to Other Sections

- **01_operating-system/**  
  Defines *how* control artifacts are used

- **03_programs/**  
  Maps academic commitments into the control cadence

- **04_projects/**  
  Connects control artifacts to external repos

- **decisions/**  
  Stores outcomes of reviews, not dashboards themselves

This folder defines *control concepts*, not implementations.

---

## Implementation Philosophy

The control layer follows three principles:

1. **Minimalism**  
   If an artifact is not used, it is removed.

2. **Replaceability**  
   Any control artifact can be replaced without system impact.

3. **Forgiveness**  
   Missed updates do not break the system.

The system must survive imperfect weeks.

---

## Change Discipline

Control artifacts are expected to change.

- New artifacts may be introduced experimentally
- Ineffective artifacts are removed without ceremony
- Successful patterns may be stabilized over time

No artifact is permanent by default.

---

## Guiding Statement

> **Visibility enables trust.**  
> **Simplicity enables use.**  
> **Control exists to serve execution, not constrain it.**

This layer exists to keep complex work moving forward with clarity and calm.
